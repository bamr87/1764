# 1849

> All things 1849 — history, events, culture, discoveries, and people from the year 1849.

## What is 1849?

1849 was a turbulent, transformative year near the middle of the 19th century: revolutions across
Europe reached their reckoning, the California Gold Rush drew the world westward, and free trade,
modern medicine, and Romantic art each passed a milestone. This repository is a curated, self-growing
collection of knowledge, references, and resources about everything related to the year **1849**.

## Topics

- **History & Politics** — World events, revolutions, treaties, and political shifts
- **Science & Discovery** — Inventions, publications, and scientific milestones
- **Arts & Culture** — Literature, music, architecture, and art
- **Economics** — Trade, legislation, and commerce
- **People** — Notable births, deaths, and figures of the era

## Notable Events of 1849

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| California Gold Rush | About 90,000 "forty-niners" flood California seeking gold in 1849, accelerating U.S. westward expansion and the road to statehood. |
| Hungarian Revolution crushed | Austria, aided by Russian military intervention, defeats Hungary's bid for independence in 1849, restoring Habsburg control over the kingdom. |
| Annexation of the Punjab | Britain annexes the Punjab on 29 March 1849 after the Second Anglo-Sikh War, completing its conquest of the Sikh Empire. |
| Roman Republic falls | French troops restore Pope Pius IX in July 1849, crushing the short-lived Roman Republic and setting back Italian unification. |
| Zachary Taylor inaugurated | Mexican-American War hero Zachary Taylor becomes the 12th U.S. president in March 1849, taking office amid deepening sectional tension over slavery. |
| Department of the Interior created | The United States establishes the Department of the Interior on 3 March 1849, centralizing management of domestic and public-land affairs. |
| Safety pin patented | Walter Hunt patents the safety pin on 10 April 1849, an enduring everyday fastener whose rights he sold off for a modest sum. |
| SS *California* reaches San Francisco | The SS *California* arrives 28 February 1849, inaugurating regular Pacific Mail steamship service to Gold Rush California. |
| First aerial bombardment | Austria launches unmanned incendiary balloons against Venice in July 1849, an early precursor of aerial warfare. |
| Frédéric Chopin dies | The Polish Romantic composer and piano virtuoso dies in Paris on 17 October 1849, leaving a defining body of works for solo piano. |
| Edgar Allan Poe dies | The American master of Gothic and detective fiction dies in Baltimore on 7 October 1849 under circumstances still debated today. |
| British Navigation Acts repealed | Britain repeals the Navigation Acts in 1849, a landmark embrace of free trade dismantling nearly two centuries of mercantilist shipping law. |
| Elizabeth Blackwell earns medical degree | On 23 January 1849 Blackwell becomes the first woman to earn a medical degree in the United States, opening the profession to women. |
| Harriet Tubman escapes slavery | Tubman flees enslavement in Maryland for Philadelphia in autumn 1849, beginning her path to becoming the Underground Railroad's foremost conductor. |
| Ivan Pavlov born | The Russian physiologist and future Nobel laureate, famed for classical conditioning, is born on 26 September 1849 in Ryazan. |
